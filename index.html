Muqami Newsletter — March 2024

Message from Mohtamim Muqami

Asalamualaikum Warahmatullah my Dear Khuddam and Atfal Brothers,


We have the blessed opportunity to once again observe the month of Ramadan. May Allah the Almighty give us the capacity to take full advantage of this blessed and holy month.


As we fast during this month, I want to remind you all of the profound guidance of the Promised Messiah (as): “Fasting is not merely staying hungry and thirsty; rather its reality and its impact can only be gained through experience. It is human nature that the less one eats, the more one’s spirit is purified and thus his capacity for [spiritual] visions increases. The will of God is to decrease one kind of sustenance and to increase the other. A person who is fasting should always be mindful that he is not just required to stay hungry. On the contrary, he should remain engaged in the remembrance of God so that he can cut asunder ties of worldly desires and amusements and is wholly devoted to God. Hence, the significance of fasting is this alone that man gives up one kind of sustenance which only nourishes the body and attains the other kind of sustenance which is a source of comfort and gratification for the soul.”


Beloved Huzoor-e-Anwar (aba) guides us: “When fasting is based on taqwa (righteousness) it produces a beautiful society, creating a spirit of sacrifice for each other. One is drawn to the needs of one’s under-privileged brothers and this is very important because it was the blessed model of the Holy Prophet(sa) that during Ramadan his alms-giving and charity would gain intense momentum like a gale storm. This becomes a source of removing anxiety from society and creates feelings of empathy for the less fortunate among those who are well-off; and feelings of love and gratefulness in the hearts of under-privileged believers for their well-off brothers.”


I wish to remind all of you, my dear brothers, to focus particularly in these final days of Ramadan on your Salat, on your Tahajjud prayers, on your recitation of the Holy Quran, on your financial sacrifices and on your reading of the treasures that are the books of the Promised Messiah (aba).


As you search for Laylatul Qadr in these final 10 nights of Ramadan, I remind you that Hazrat Aisha (ra) asked the Holy Prophet (saw): “O Messenger of Allah! If I realize Lailat Al-Qadr (The Night of Decree), what should I supplicate in it?'' He (saw) replied,: “You should supplicate: Allahumma innaka `afuwun, tuhibbul-`afwa, fa`fu `anni” (O Allah, You are Most Forgiving, and You love forgiveness; so forgive me)


May Allah allow this month to be a means of spiritual and moral rejuvenation for all of the Khuddam and Atfal in the Muqami Majlis.


Humbly requesting your prayers.


Wasalam

Najeeb Chaudhry

March Recap

Monthly Khuddam Meeting

At the monthly Khuddam meeting, the air was charged with earnest discussion and deep reflection. The conditions of Bai'at were thoughtfully examined, instilling a renewed commitment to its principles. As the Khuddam pledge echoed through the room, there was a palpable sense of resolve to uphold its tenets. The session was not just about revisiting foundational commitments but also about reinforcing the spirit of unity and dedication amongst the attendees.

Flag Football

Despite a muddy pitch, Muqami Khuddam played with unwavering spirit in the flag football event. Their dedication shone as they tackled the slippery field with vigor, embodying teamwork and resilience. The challenging conditions only strengthened their bonds, as they cheered each other on, celebrating every goal and effort. It was a true display of brotherhood and sportsmanship that will be remembered.

Sehri with Respected Sadr MKA

The Sehri event was a harmonious blend of fellowship and spirituality. As dawn approached, Muqami Khuddam gathered at IHOP, sharing the pre-dawn meal with respected Sadr MKA amidst warm discussions. The early morning ambiance added a serene touch to their camaraderie. Following the meal, they proceeded to Bait-ur-Rahman, where respected Sadr MKA delivered an insightful sermon that resonated with the hearts of all present. It was an enriching start to the day, filled with reflection and a strengthened sense of community.
